Train Problems - Key Formulas

Train Passing a Stationary Object

Time = Length of train / Speed of train

t = L / S

Train Passing a Platform/Station

Time = (Length of train + Length of platform) / Speed of train

t = (Ltrain + Lplatform) / S

Two Trains Crossing Each Other

Same Direction: Time = (L1 + L2) / |S1 - S2|

Opposite Direction: Time = (L1 + L2) / (S1 + S2)

Speed Conversion

km/hr to m/s: Multiply by 5/18

m/s to km/hr: Multiply by 18/5

1 km/hr = 5/18 m/s

Train Problems

Practice aptitude questions on trains, speed, time, and distance

Questions answered: 0/60

Question 1:

A train with a speed of 120kmph passes a man in 15 seconds. Find the length of the train.

  • A) 200m
  • B) 300m
  • C) 400m
  • D) 500m
Explanation:

The speed of the train in meter per second = 120 * 1000 / 3600 = 120 * 5 / 18 = 600 / 18 = 100 / 3 m/s
The time taken by the train to travel through the man = 15 seconds
The length of the train = 100 * 15 / 3 = 500 m.

Question 2:

A train with a speed of 45kmph passes a man in 20 seconds. Find the length of the train.

  • A) 150m
  • B) 200m
  • C) 250m
  • D) 300m
Explanation:

The speed of the train in meters per second = 45 * 5 / 18 = 25 / 2 m/s
The time taken by the train to pass through the man = 20 seconds
The length of the train = 20 * 25 / 2 = 250m.

Question 3:

A train with length of 50 m passes a man in 3 seconds. Find the speed of the train.

  • A) 15 kmph
  • B) 30kmph
  • C) 45kmph
  • D) 60kmph
Explanation:

The distance covered by the train = the length of the train
The distance covered = 50m
The time taken = 3 seconds
The speed = 50 / 3 m/s = 50 / 3 * 3600 / 1000 = 50 * 18 / (5 * 3) = 60 kmph.

Question 4:

A train 150 m in length passes a man in 12 seconds. Find the speed of the train.

  • A) 40 kmph
  • B) 45 kmph
  • C) 50 kmph
  • D) 55 kmph
Explanation:

The distance to be covered = the length of the train
The distance to be covered = 150m
The time taken = 12 seconds
The speed = 150 / 12 = 25 / 2 m/s
The speed = 25 / 2 * 18 / 5 kmph = 45 kmph.

Question 5:

The speed of a train is 120 kmph and the length of the train is 240 m. Find the time it will take to cross a pole.

  • A) 2.4 seconds
  • B) 4.8 seconds
  • C) 7.2 seconds
  • D) 9.6 seconds
Explanation:

The distance to be covered = 240 m
The speed = 120 kmph
The speed in meter per second = 120 * 5 / 18 = 100 / 3
The time taken = 240 / 100 * 3 = 7.2 seconds.

Question 6:

A train with length 300m crosses a pole at a speed of 50 kmph. Find the time taken by the train to cross the pole.

  • A) 20.6 seconds
  • B) 21.6 seconds
  • C) 22.6 seconds
  • D) 23.6 seconds
Explanation:

The speed of the train = 50 kmph = 50 * 5 / 18 meter per second = 125 / 9 m/s
The distance to be covered = 300 m
The time taken to cross 300m = 300 / 125 * 9 = 108 / 5 seconds = 21.6 seconds.

Question 7:

A train is 315 m in length and crosses a pole in 27 seconds. Find the speed of the train.

  • A) 42 kmph
  • B) 44 kmph
  • C) 46 kmph
  • D) 48 kmph
Explanation:

The distance to be covered = the length of the train
The distance to be covered = 315 m
The time taken = 27 seconds
The speed of the train = 315 / 27 m/s = 35 / 3 m/s = 35 / 3 * (18 / 5) = 42 kmph.

Question 8:

A train 405 m in length passes a pole in 45 seconds. Find the speed of the train.

  • A) 32.4 kmph
  • B) 23.4 kmph
  • C) 34.3 kmph
  • D) 24.3 kmph
Explanation:

The distance to be covered = the length of the train
The distance to be covered = 405 m
The time taken = 45 seconds
The speed of the train = 405 / 45 = 9 m/s
The speed = 9 * 18 / 5 = 32.4 kmph.

Question 9:

A train moving at a speed of 25 kmph crosses a pole in 9 seconds. Find the length of the train.

  • A) 60 m
  • B) 62.5 m
  • C) 65 m
  • D) 70 m
Explanation:

The length of the train = the distance covered
The distance covered = speed * time = 25 * 9 / 3600 km = 25 * 5 / 18 * 9 m = 62.5 m.

Question 10:

A train covers 120 km in 480 minutes. Find the speed of the train.

  • A) 3.16 m/s
  • B) 4.16 m/s
  • C) 5.16 m/s
  • D) 6.16 m/s
Explanation:

The speed of the train = distance / time = 120 / 480 = 0.25 km/minute = 15 kmph
The speed in meter/second = 15 * 5 / 18 = 4.16 m/s.

Question 11:

A train crosses a station of 400 m in 20 seconds running at a speed of 198 kmph. Find the length of the train.

  • A) 600 m
  • B) 700 m
  • C) 800 m
  • D) 900 m
Explanation:

The speed of the train in meter per second = 55 m/s (198 * 5 / 18)
The distance covered by the train = 55 * 20 = 1100 m
The length of the train = total distance covered – the length of the platform = 1100 – 400 = 700 m

Question 12:

A train 500 m in length crosses a station in 25 seconds travelling at a speed of 144 kmph. Find the length of the station.

  • A) 400 m
  • B) 500 m
  • C) 600 m
  • D) 700 m
Explanation:

The distance covered = time * speed = 25 * 40 = 1000 m (144 kmph = 40 m/s)
The length of the station = total distance – length of the train = 1000 – 500 = 500 m

Question 20:

A train passes a platform in 49 seconds. Find the length of the train if the length of the platform is 150 m and the speed of the train is 79.2 kmph.

  • A) 988 m
  • B) 978 m
  • C) 948 m
  • D) 928 m
Explanation:

The total distance = speed * time = 49 * 22 = 1078 m
The length of the train = 1078 – 150 = 928 m
