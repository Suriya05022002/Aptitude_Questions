Train Problems - Key Formulas

Train Passing a Stationary Object

Time = Length of train / Speed of train

t = L / S

Train Passing a Platform/Station

Time = (Length of train + Length of platform) / Speed of train

t = (Ltrain + Lplatform) / S

Two Trains Crossing Each Other

Same Direction: Time = (L1 + L2) / |S1 - S2|

Opposite Direction: Time = (L1 + L2) / (S1 + S2)

Speed Conversion

km/hr to m/s: Multiply by 5/18

m/s to km/hr: Multiply by 18/5

1 km/hr = 5/18 m/s

Question 1:

A train with a speed of 120kmph passes a man in 15 seconds. Find the length of the train.

  • A) 200m
  • B) 300m
  • C) 400m
  • D) 500m
Explanation:

The speed of the train in meter per second = 120 * 1000 / 3600 = 120 * 5 / 18 = 600 / 18 = 100 / 3 m/s
The time taken by the train to travel through the man = 15 seconds
The length of the train = 100 * 15 / 3 = 500 m.

Question 2:

A train with a speed of 45kmph passes a man in 20 seconds. Find the length of the train.

  • A) 150m
  • B) 200m
  • C) 250m
  • D) 300m
Explanation:

The speed of the train in meters per second = 45 * 5 / 18 = 25 / 2 m/s
The time taken by the train to pass through the man = 20 seconds
The length of the train = 20 * 25 / 2 = 250m.

Question 3:
